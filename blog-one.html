MSEDCL hikes electricity tariff effective 1st april for all categories

Starting 1st April 2024 the fixed costs for customers in various categories will be increased compared to April 1, 2023.

Starting 1st April 2024 the fixed costs for customers in various categories will be increased compared to April 1, 2023.

The domestic category for single phase will be charged at Rs.128 instead or Rs.116.

Three Phase category will be charged at Rs.445 instead of Rs.385

Commercial charges will be Rs.517 instead of Rs.467 for up to 5 horsepower

Public water supply customers will see an increase from Rs.117 to Rs.129 for 1 to 20 kilowatts, and from Rs.142 to Rs.156 for 20 to 40 kW. For 40 kW the cost will rise from Rs.137 to Rs.194.

Agricultural customers without meters will have to pay Rs.563 instead of Rs.467 for up to 5 horsepower.

Small industrial customers up to 20 kW will be charged Rs.583 instead of Rs.530.

Street lights will cost Rs.142 instead of Rs.129.

Government offices and hospitals will have a fixed rate of Rs.427 instead of Rs.388 for 20 kW

As per MERC guidelines, the MSEDCL tariff are effective from 1st of April 2024 and will be reflected in the April bills.

Maharashtra to start voluntary linking of Aadhaar with land records

Maharashtra is set to begin voluntary linking of Aadhaar with land records to ensure transparency, curb fraudulent practices, and reduce land disputes.

The process is expected to start within 100days after the Lok Sabha elections. The process will be initiated by the state director of e-mutation project. Proposals from Maharashtra, Rajasthan, and Gujarat are under consideration and expected to begin by end of the current year or early next financial year. The consent-based Aadhaar linkage aims to ensure transparency and give real time data for land related services across sectors, ensuring citizen-centric services and ease of living. The Centre will aggressively push for both Aadhaar seeding and ULPIN or Bhu-Aadhaar in land governance in the coming days. The voluntary seeding will help determine the total number of land parcels with an individual. The state has already rolled out the Unique Land Parcel Identification Number (ULPIN) or Bhu-Aadhaar to identify every surveyed land parcel.
